Potal bumping section

Portal bumping

Portal bumping is a very simple, but extremely useful glitch in Portal. It uses a mechanic which was implemented to negate frustrating portal shots. When you shoot a portal at an already existing one the new one appears next to it. The only part missed by the devs, is that given 2 portalable surfaces and a wall separating them you can bypass the wall with this mechanic. This can save a lot of time on chamber 15, 16 and 17, and also allows you to never wake up the rocket turret in the Escape Sequence, altough that is a slower route.

ABH section

Accelarated backwards hopping

ABH is one of the few glitches that doesn't originate from the games use of portals but the Source engine itself, which the game was made in. It is one of the fastest ways to navigate the chambers of Aperture Science other than using the portal gun and other glitches. It uses backwards jumping and strafing familiar to those who played any of Valve's titles. Funnily enough a chamber such as Chamber 8 can be solved faster with this movement than with using portals.

Save glitch section

Save glitch

The Save glitch is probably the most important and also most complicated glitch of the game, some say it could be a PhD research in and of itself. The base principle is that if you save-load while partially inside a portal the game gets very confused and the following things can be achieved:

  • You can maintain a portal's collision state, meaning you can walk through walls and doors making some chambers extremely trivial.
  • You can also maintain a button's „pressed” state, allowing you to finish chambers before having access to cubes.
  • You can separate the player's body from the camera, allowing for seemingly random shots solving entire chambers with just a few well placed portals.

This glitch, altough it may seem OoB, it is actually inbounds because of the rules set in the speedrunning community: Beat the game without having any props, your camera, placed portals or traveling portals leave the map. When it's performed right it's one of the craziest glitches in not only Portal but gaming in general. Sadly it was removed in the sequel.

the cake is a lie

The engine Portal is running on is the Source engine which is known for causing absurd glitches that never get fixed. Why Valve decided to leave these in the games is an interesting question but we know for sure that they can range from extremely funny to frustrating and most importantly for speedrunning, crazy fast. Most glitches in Portal are caused by the portals themselves but in conjunction with saving and loading they cause the most absurd ones like the Save glitch.
